Technical Approach

Swashplateless by design

Conventional helicopters control blade pitch with a swashplate — mechanically complex, heavy, and another point of failure. Excalibur replaces it with inclined lag-pitch coupling hinges set 50° from vertical. Independent torque modulation on each rotor's electric motor drives a lead-lag oscillation that passively couples into cyclic pitch control — an approach grounded in rotorcraft research from the University of Pennsylvania.

  • Dual electric motors with independent torque control
  • Belt-drive reduction
  • Three-plate carbon fiber frame
  • Custom flight controller firmware for swashplateless control
The Mission

DARPA Heavy Lift Challenge

The LIFT Challenge asks for a VTOL aircraft that can carry four times its own weight — a capability that doesn't exist today. Solving it opens new missions in logistics, emergency response, and remote operations, where traditional helicopters are too heavy and drones can't carry enough.
